Secretariat & Directors

Philip Pan

Secretary-General


Hello Delegates, Faculty Advisors, and Friends! Welcome to TechMUN XXVIII! My name is Philip Pan and it is my pleasure to be serving as the Secretary-General of TechMUN this year. I joined Model UN as a freshman and it has been an essential part of my life ever since. Outside of MUN, you can find me working on my startup, catching up on must-read books, and delegating to my favorite USG Evan Williams.


Running TechMUN last year as the Director of General Staff was one of the best experiences of my life, and I’m extremely thrilled to be a part of it again for my final year in high school. Though my love for competing in Specialized Agencies is great, I’ve had an even better time working to set this conference up, and I’m excited to see the discussion and cooperation that you all will foster during TechMUN weekend. We pride ourselves on creating a conference experience where we push one another to step outside our comfort zones and grow as individuals. It’s my hope that we can create the same environment for all of our attendees.


Thank you all for considering TechMUN XXVIII. I look forward to meeting you all on April 24! Please feel free to email tjmodelun@gmail.com with any questions.

Rhoshini Raghuraman

Director of Delegations


Hello Delegates and Faculty Advisors! If you haven’t already gotten many emails from me about TechMUN XXVIII, allow me to formally introduce myself. I’m Rhoshini Raghuraman and I will be serving as your Director of Delegations for this conference. I’m thrilled that you all have decided to compete at TechMUN with passion, dedication, and enthusiasm that truly embody the Model UN spirit. MUN has quickly become my favorite activity in high school, from getting in heated debates over federal direct investments in committee to just exploring cities like Philly at national conferences. When I’m not writing my 11-page idea papers, you can catch me doing last-minute homework (yes, as a second semester senior) while watching any new show that pops up on Hulu, making nanoparticles, or going on gastronomical adventures with friends.


As for TechMUN, I challenge you all to treat this conference as a tabula rasa. Do anything you want when you want: go give that speech without any notes, go lead that large group of people, and go write that all-encompassing, multifaceted resolution paper. I hope TechMUN is a blank slate in your MUN career, whether it be your first or your last conference. During the weekend, I also know it will be stressful at times, so don’t be afraid to make a joke or two in your committees, and have some fun yourself. I know that I will.

Lisa Raj

Director of General Staff


Delegates, welcome to TechMUN XXVIII! My name is Lisa Raj, and I’m absolutely thrilled to serve as your Director of General Staff this year. After joining MUN in 7th grade, it’s become such an integral part of my life, to the point where people like to tell me I have no life:) Jokes aside, MUN has provided me with some of my most cherished experiences, from competing in all types of committee from GA’s to crisis, helping TJMUN novices with last minute revisions to position papers, and meeting new people from around the country at national conferences. I strongly encourage each of you to take TechMUN as an opportunity to bring yourself out of your comfort zone, and try something new - whether it be raising your placard for the first time or writing a private directive. Feel more than welcome to wave hello, introduce yourself and share your experiences or a funny story whenever you see me! You’ll be sure to find me dropping by each committee room including yours to ensure your chairs, directors, and staffers have everything they need to provide you with the best TechMUN experience possible. I look forward to meeting you all! If at any time you have any questions or concerns, feel free to email tjmodelun@gmail.com.
